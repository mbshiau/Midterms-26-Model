Objective

Develop a full-stack web application that automatically aggregates polling data for the 2026 Pennsylvania gubernatorial election, runs probabilistic election simulations, and presents an interactive forecast to users.

Unlike a polling average, the application estimates uncertainty by running Monte Carlo simulations and reports:

projected vote share
probability of victory
distribution of likely outcomes

Future versions may support multiple races, demographic modeling, and AI-generated explanations.


Goals
Primary Goals
Automatically ingest new polling data
Maintain a clean polling database
Generate probabilistic election forecasts
Visualize uncertainty instead of only averages
Deploy as a public web application
Secondary Goals
Historical forecast tracking
AI-generated forecast explanations
Pollster reliability weighting
User-defined simulation scenarios
